Presenter Information

This page provides information to presenters at INLG about how to prepare your recorded presentations.

Presentations must be submitted via July 8th 11:59 pm AOE.

Oral Presenters

  • May submit a 1 minute video preview, accompanied by a closed caption file.
  • Must submit a video presentation of 10 to 15 minutes, accompanied by a closed caption file and a pdf file of slides.

Poster Presenters

  • May submit a 1 minute video preview, accompanied by a closed caption file.
  • Must submit a video presentation of 10 to 15 minutes, accompanied by a closed caption file and a pdf poster file of no larger than 3 ft wide by 5 ft tall.

Demo Presenters

  • May submit a 1 minute video preview, accompanied by a closed caption file.
  • Must submit a video presentation of 10 to 15 minutes, accompanied by a closed caption file and at least one of the following:
    • a pdf poster file of no larger than 3 ft wide by 5 ft tall.
    • a pdf file of slides.

INLG 2022 is a Hybrid Conference

Each paper to be presented will have a card on the conference website, linking to:

  • The video preview (if any)
  • The video presentation
  • The poster and/or slides
  • A discord channel, for Q&A

These materials will be available to both in-person and remote conference attendees.

Live presentations and live Q&A will be done through zoom / discord, during the conference.

Presenters are encouraged to upload recordings and slides/posters to the ACL Anthology after the conference.

File Naming Convention

  • All files (videos, subtitles, images) are uploaded through OpenReview. please use the following guidance when naming your submission materials.
  • Name all files with the following convention: [EventPrefix]_[PaperID]_[SubmissionItem].[ext].
  • For the the [EventPrefix] please use: INLG_oral, INLG_poster, INLG_demo or INLG_genchal.

Technical Information for Recordings

Your submission consists of two pieces:

1. Video Preview

  • A 1-minute preview video that clearly communicates the research and contribution, inviting readers to read your paper.
  • Must be self-contained.
  • Subtitles are mandatory to increase accessibility. See below for tips on how to create and include subtitles
  • File Name: [EventPrefix]_[PaperID]_[FirstAuthorLastName]_Preview.mp4
  • Example: INLG_oral_001_Preview.mp4

2. Presentation Video

  • Must follow the formatting described below.
  • Subtitles are mandatory to increase accessibility. See below for tips on how to create and include subtitles
  • Example: INLG_poster_002_Presentation.mp4

Guidance for creating both and requirements for file encoding and naming are provided below. Videos will be hosted on Vimeo during July-August 2022.

Recorded Videos Format

All video submissions must meet the following requirements:

  • 1920x1080 resolution at 30FPS
  • Maximum Size:
    • 100MB for preview videos.
    • 500MB for paper presentation videos.
  • MPEG-4 using H.264 encoding (file extension is .mp4)
  • Content: Please ensure you have copyright or a license to all content included in recordings.
  • Verification:
    • To encode/re-encode your video in the right format, you can use the free software Handbrake
    • To check that your video is in the right format, you can use the free software MediaInfo

Recording Your Talk

We recommend using OBS Studio to record your presentation, which is free and cross-platform. We do not recommend using Powerpoint’s built in recording since the recording is done per-slide, so if you speak during a slide transition the audio will not be recorded. These issues can be tough to catch and fix.

After recording your talk, please rewatch it to ensure the video and audio are recorded correctly.

Mandatory: Providing Subtitles with Your Video

There are a number of tools that are available to generate captions for you, which you can download and include with your video. Among the free options, YouTube, Veed.io, and Facebook, for example, have free tools that automatically create closed captions and make it possible to download these.

Please note two important things for this:

  1. in general it takes some time to generate the files and needs correction (particularly with uncommon words or terminology), so you should take this into account so that the video is finished before the deadline;
  2. if you use a social media platform to generate subtitles, please do not publish the video completely (not even private or unlisted) as it will interfere with the conference and will increase the work on the student volunteers and might lead to delays.

There are also a number of paid services that enable the transcription of videos, such as Amberscript. Lastly, it is also possible to manually create the subtitles using for example the YouTube captioning tool, Adobe Premiere Pro, or Amara. Regardless of the tool you are using, please make sure that the subtitles are accurate and correctly timed for the benefit of all attendees. Videos containing subtitles have a considerably higher engagement and retention rate than one’s without.

Include the captions file with your video, following the same naming convention: SBV or SRT formatted captions are acceptable.

You can test your subtitles by playing your video in VLC with the .sbv/.srt file in the same directory so that VLC will find it. Then right-click the VLC window and select the subtitle menu item to pick your subtitles to play during your video.
